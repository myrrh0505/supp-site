How to treat Laryngopharyngeal Reflux (LPR)

What is LPR?

Lpr or often called silent reflux is very similar to GERD, however the reflux contents travels higher all the way up to the voice box and even the nasal passage. Often patients do not feel heartburn. However when the reflux contents reaches the esophagus and voice box it creates irritation. Normally it is very hard to diagnose someone with LPR due to confusion with respiratory symptoms. The most common symptoms in adults include: mucous being stuck in the throat, hoarseness, constant clearing mucous, coughing, having to spit out mucous, post-nasal drip, difficulties breathing, and sleeping issues due the build up of mucous in throat.
